C0185 Farm Animals #The Weekend

A
Isn’t this great? I always wanted to own a farm, live out in the country, grow my own food!
B
This is very beautiful. Though I have to confess, I don’t know the first thing about farming!
A
That’s fine! Don’t worry about it!
B
What was that?
A
Relax, it was just a goat!
B
And that?
A
It’s just the cows that are grazing over there. We can milk them later.
B
What was that?
A
Honey, seriously, It’s just a sheep. Relax!
A
Relax, that was just the horses and donkeys that are in the stable .
B
You know what? I don’t think I can hack it here out in the countryside. I’m going back to the city!

Key Vocabulary

country common noun, singular rural areas
graze principle verb, present progressive to feed in a meadow or pasture
milk principle verb, infinitive get the milk from a cow
don’t know the first thing about phrase know little about
stable common noun, singular a building for the shelter and feeding of domestic animals, especially horses and cattle
hack it principle verb, present simple be able to manage or manage successfully

Supplementary Vocabulary

stall common noun, singular a compartment for one domestic animal in a barn or shed
pig sty common noun, singular a pen or enclosure for pigs; pigpen
pen common noun, singular a small enclosure for domestic animals
chicken coop common noun, singular an enclosure or cage for chickens
herd common noun, singular a group of cattle or other domestic animals of a single kind kept together
